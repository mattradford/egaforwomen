How To Visit

The Elizabeth Garrett Anderson Gallery is located at the UNISON Centre, 130 Euston Road, NW1 2AY, between Euston Station and the British Library.

It is now open to the public between 9:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. on Wednesdays, Thursdays and Fridays, as well as 9:00am to 4:00pm on the third Saturday of each month. Entrance is free. Although the exhibition is interactive and self explanatory if you want information about the gallery this may be provided by arrangement with EGA for Women.

If you want to bring a group of 10 or more people to visit, please contact facilitieshelpdesk@unison.co.uk.They need to know dates, numbers, who the organisation is and what it represents. There are no catering facilities available although plenty of cafés nearby. Enjoy your visit.

Getting There

As you stroll along the Museum Mile between the Wellcome Museum and the British Library, enter the vast atrium of the modern UNISON Centre on the North side of the Euston Road and simply bear left into the main ground floor rooms of the hospital where the permanent exhibition is located. Once inside, experience the ‘domestic’ nature of the original building and its comforting welcome to women.

EGA for Women in partnership with UNISON has restored the Elizabeth Garrett Hospital for Women to its full Victorian splendour. Central to this beautiful building with its many original late nineteenth century features is the Elizabeth Garrett Anderson Gallery which is freely available to the public as a state of the art Gallery.
